# Coffer

**A clear, calm way to see where your money goes.**

Coffer is a personal budgeting app that goes beyond a simple expense log. It
understands that your bills don't all land the same way — some hit every
month, some once a quarter, some once a year — and it builds your whole
picture of income, spending, and savings around that, for whichever month
you want to look at.

---

## Overview

Sign in with your Google account and Coffer becomes your personal, private
budget — your income, your expenses, and your categories, visible only to
you. Everything is organized around **recurring entries** rather than
one-off transactions: you tell Coffer what you earn and what you spend on a
repeating basis, and it works out what that actually means for any given
month, automatically.

---

## Key Features

- **A real multi-month budget**, not just a snapshot of today
- Recurring expenses and income with proper start/end dates
- A dashboard that adapts to whichever month you're browsing
- Category-based spending breakdown with charts
- Automatic UK income tax, National Insurance, and pension estimates
- CSV import and export for both expenses and income
- Dark and light themes, with a mobile-first design throughout
- Toast confirmations, loading skeletons, and helpful empty states — the
  small details that make an app feel finished

---

## Dashboard (Home)

The Home screen is Coffer's central view — a full picture of one month at a
glance, without feeling cluttered:

- **Month picker** — step backward or forward through time, or jump
  straight back to the current month
- **Summary cards** — net income, monthly spend, remaining budget, and
  savings rate for the month you're viewing
- **Budget usage bar** — a visual read on how much of your income this
  month's spending has used up, with a warning color as you approach or
  exceed it
- **Category breakdown** — a pie chart showing exactly what you're spending
  on this month
- **Income vs. expenses** — a simple side-by-side comparison
- **This month's expenses** — everything that's actually landing in the
  selected month, biggest first
- **Insights** — a few automatic, plain-English observations: your biggest
  spending category, whether you're in the black or the red this month, and
  how your savings rate is trending

## Multi-Month & Historical Views

This is the heart of Coffer: every number on the dashboard is calculated
fresh for whichever month you've selected, rather than being locked to
"right now."

- A **monthly** expense only shows up from its start date onward (and stops
  after its end date, if it has one)
- A **yearly** expense — like an annual subscription or insurance renewal —
  only appears as a lump sum in the specific month it's due, not spread
  thinly across all twelve
- A **quarterly** expense appears once every three months from when it
  started
- **Daily, weekly, and fortnightly** expenses are averaged against the
  actual number of days in whichever month you're viewing, so February
  isn't treated the same as a 31-day month

Nothing is duplicated behind the scenes to make this work — Coffer simply
recalculates what belongs in each month from your recurring entries every
time you look. Browse back to January to see what your finances looked
like, or forward to a future month to see what's coming up.

## Expense Management

- Add, edit, and remove expenses, with a confirmation step before anything
  is deleted for good
- Organize by **category**, each with its own color
- Choose a **frequency**: Daily, Weekly, Fortnightly, Monthly, Quarterly, or
  Yearly
- Optional **start and end dates** to control exactly when a recurring
  expense is active
- Optional **notes** for anything worth remembering about an entry
- **Sort** your list — A→Z, Z→A, highest amount, or lowest amount
- Switch between **List**, **Card**, or **Group by Category** views
- Tap any entry for a full **details view** — every field at a glance,
  read-only

---

## Income Management

- Track **multiple income sources** at once, each with its own effective
  date range
- Categorize each source: Main Job, Secondary Job, Self-employed, Business,
  Freelance, Side Hustle, Investments, Rental Income, Pension, Benefits,
  Gifts, or Other — plus an optional free-text detail (employer, client,
  property, account — whatever's relevant)
- The same **sort**, **card/list view**, and **Group by Source** options as
  Expenses
- A full **details view** for every income entry
- Automatic **UK tax, National Insurance, and pension** estimates, so your
  "net income" figure reflects what actually lands in your pocket

## Import & Export

Coffer defines its own simple CSV format for both expenses and income —
independent of any particular spreadsheet, so it's easy to prepare in
Excel, Google Sheets, Numbers, or a plain text editor.

- **Export** your expenses or income to a CSV file at any time
- **Import** a CSV back in, with a clear preview before anything is
  written: how many entries will be added, and specific reasons for any
  rows that couldn't be understood (a missing category, an invalid amount,
  an unrecognized frequency)
- Unknown or extra columns in an imported file are simply ignored, so your
  own notes or calculations in a spreadsheet won't cause problems
- Calculated figures (like a yearly total) are included on export for your
  reference, but are always recalculated rather than re-imported as data

## Personalization

- **Currency** — USD, GBP, or EUR
- **Date and number formatting** — choose the convention that matches how
  you read dates and figures
- **Theme** — Dark, Light, or Match System, with Dark as the default

---

## Design & Mobile Experience

Coffer is built mobile-first:

- A bottom navigation bar keeps Home, quick-add, and Settings within thumb's
  reach
- A one-tap **quick-add** button for logging an expense from anywhere in
  the app
- Comfortable touch targets throughout, rather than small tap-and-hope
  buttons
- An add/edit form that slides up naturally on a phone, rather than a
  centered pop-up that's awkward to reach one-handed
- Toast notifications confirm what just happened; loading skeletons show
  where content is on its way; empty states offer guidance instead of a
  blank screen

---

## Sign-In & Privacy

Coffer uses Google Sign-In. Your income, expenses, and settings are private
to your account — nobody else can see them.
